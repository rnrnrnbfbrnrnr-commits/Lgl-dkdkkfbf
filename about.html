About Coverage Ledger

Coverage Ledger publishes plain-English guides to insurance — life, auto, home, and health — aimed at readers who want to understand a policy before they need to file a claim, not after.

Why we exist

Insurance documents are written by lawyers for regulators, not for the people buying coverage. That leaves a gap between what a policy says and what most people think it says — a gap that usually only becomes visible during a claim, when it's too late to fix. We write guides that close that gap in advance.

How we work

Our editorial process starts from primary sources: sample policy forms, state insurance department filings, and public complaint data, rather than insurer marketing pages. Every guide is written to a single standard — plain sentences, consistent definitions, and an explicit section on the mistakes that most commonly delay or reduce a claim.

What we are not

Coverage Ledger is a publisher, not a licensed insurance agency, broker, or advisor. Nothing on this site is individualized financial, legal, or insurance advice. Coverage details, pricing, and availability vary by insurer, state, and individual circumstances — always confirm specifics against your actual policy documents or with a licensed professional before making a coverage decision.
